# KloudeSIM brand voice

## Who we sound like

A well-travelled mate who happens to know the eSIM market inside out. Direct, practical, slightly opinionated. We're the challenger brand against Airalo and Holafly — we earn trust by being more honest and more specific than they are, not by shouting.

- UK English throughout.
- Contractions always: it's, you'll, don't, we've.
- Second person ("you'll land and connect") over corporate third person.
- Specifics over adjectives: "£8.99 for 5GB" beats "affordable plans". Prices, GB, days, network names.
- It's fine to have a view: "the 10GB plan is the one most people should buy."

## Banned phrases

seamless, hassle-free, unlock, elevate, leverage, game-changer, stay connected like never before, in today's connected world, look no further, robust, holistic, empower, your gateway to, peace of mind (max once per page, ideally zero).

## Humanising pass (run on every draft)

This is a condensed version of the KloudStack humanise-writing skill. Apply it as a deliberate editing pass after drafting.

**Surface tells — remove:**
- Filler openers ("In today's fast-paced world...", "It's important to note...")
- Hollow transitions: Furthermore / Moreover / Additionally → And, But, So, or cut
- Over-hedging ("it could be argued", "in many cases") → be specific or cut

**Structural tells — these are what readers and detectors actually catch:**
- The em-dash construction "X — it's Y". Allow at most one em dash per page.
- The two-sentence pivot: "This isn't about X. It's about Y." Never.
- 3–4 stacked short punchy sentences as performed drama. Mix lengths instead.
- Perfectly parallel bullet lists — break one item, lengthen another.
- Every paragraph opening with a tidy topic sentence. Start one mid-thought or with an example.
- Rhetorical questions that immediately answer themselves.
- Conclusions that tie a neat bow. Just stop when the point's made.

**Add:**
- Varied sentence rhythm: one notably long sentence and one very short one per major section.
- One genuine-reaction moment per page ("which honestly surprised us when we tested it", "Holafly's throttling policy is buried three clicks deep, which tells you something").
- At least one sentence starting with And, But, or So.

## What never changes

- Prices, data amounts, validity periods, network names — exactly as per the CSV.
- Legal/regulatory statements.
- The comparison table's honesty. If we lose on a row, we lose on the row and say why it might not matter.
